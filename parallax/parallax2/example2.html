About Charlotte, North Carolina

History

North Carolina was one of the original thirteen colonies founded by Sir Walter Raleigh. Originally North and South Carolina were together, but in 1712 Charlotte, North Carolina was founded in 1768 by Scottish-Irish settelers. It was named after the King George III's wife Queen Charlotte which is where the city's nickname "the queen city" comes from. Charlotte is also located in the 38th largest county in North Carolina, but is the largest city in North Carolina in population and land area. In fact it is so larget that eclipses NC's capital and second largest city by almost twice in both population and area. North and South Carolina where originally a conjoined state, but split in 1712 into the states we know and love today. In 1799 the first gold was discovered at what is known as Reed's Gold Mine just outside of Charlotte.

Importance

Charlotte, NC, like many cities benefitted heavily from the inventions in the 1920's allowsing it to build the infastructor and skyscrappers we see there today. Charlotte is home to hearquarters for many big companies like IBM, Microsoft, Oracle, Bank of America, and Wells Fargo which hire students that graduate from the University of North Carolina Charlotte's Computer Science program.

Sports and Attractions

Charlotte is home to many sports teams like the Carolina Panthers(NFL), Charlotte Hornets(NBA), Charlotte Knights(Minor League Baseball), and the University of North Carolina Charlotte Forty Niners. Attractions include the NASCAR hall of fame, Carowinds Amusement Park, Discovery Place, the Billy Graham Library, and the White Water Center.
